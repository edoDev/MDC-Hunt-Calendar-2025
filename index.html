How to use this calendar

Use it offline (in the field)

After one visit with a connection, the whole app — seasons, scouting log, sun and moon data — works with no cell signal.

Install to your phone: Android/Chrome: menu (⋮) → Add to Home screen or Install app. iPhone/Safari: Share (□↑) → Add to Home Screen. It then opens full-screen like an app.

When you're offline, an OFFLINE badge appears in the toolbar with the date the season data was last verified — regulations can change, so re-open online now and then to pick up updates.

Reading the calendar

Colored bars on a day = seasons open that day. Hatched bars mean the dates are confirmed but Jefferson County's open-county status is pending MDC's 2026 Fall booklet — verify before hunting. Click any day for sunrise/sunset, legal shooting light, moon, and rules. Toggle layers in the legend, or press L to hide it. Esc closes panels.

Tags: REG = set by MDC (sourced in the footer). MODEL = computed here (sun, moon, rut estimate) — not regulatory. LOG = your own data.

Bear permit status

Pick your status in the toolbar. Permit holders get a CALL QUOTA LINE chip on bear-season days — MDC requires calling 800-668-4045 before hunting each day. Everyone sees the season, zone rules, and the May 2027 application window.

Scouting

Field Craft — reading the signals

This app shows you data so you can think like the animal, not so you can obey a number. Below is how much weight each signal actually deserves. The honest truth of hunting: a few factors decide almost everything, and the rest is noise that feels like signal.

Wind STRONG

A deer's nose is its life. It processes scent in ways that make our sense of smell look broken, and a whitetail that smells you doesn't spook once — it learns that spot and avoids it for weeks. Wind is the one variable you fully control by choosing where to sit. Play the wrong wind on your best stand and you don't just blow the sit — you educate the herd and burn the location. This is why the disciplined answer is sometimes "don't hunt today." Tag each stand with the winds that carry your scent away from where deer approach, and let the app tell you what's hunt-able.

Time of day STRONG

Deer and bear are crepuscular — built for dawn and dusk, when low light favors prey escaping predators. The prime windows the app shades are where most daylight movement happens. During the rut, midday movement rises as bucks cruise for does, which is the one time all-day sits pay off.

The rut STRONG

For a couple of weeks in November, mature bucks abandon the caution that kept them nocturnal all year. This is the single biggest predictor of daylight buck movement in the whole season — the rare case where the calendar itself is the tip.

Cold fronts & pressure MODERATE

A sharp temperature drop after a front often triggers a feeding surge, and hunters report good movement on those days. The effect is real but variable — a helpful tiebreaker between two hunt-able days, not a reason to override wind.

Moon phase WEAK

This is the honesty test. Folk wisdom and commercial apps sell moon-phase "prediction," but GPS-collar studies have largely failed to find a consistent effect on daylight movement or harvest. We show the moon because it's real and mildly useful for planning legal light and midnight feeding — not because it tells you when to hunt. If a feature promises to rank your days by moon, be skeptical.

Putting it together

Think of it as filters, not a score. First: does a stand play this wind? If no stand does, that's your answer. Then: are you there for a prime window, or the rut? Then, and only to break a tie: is a front coming? Everything else — moon, "best day" calculators, gadgets — is background. Stacking ten weak indicators doesn't make a strong one; it just makes noise you'll mistake for confidence. The only true end-all would be knowing exactly where the animal is, and no legal tool gives you that — which is why woodsmanship, not data, is still the hunter's real edge. The data just helps you ask better questions of the woods.
